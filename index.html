ABOUT

JAI SATHGURU FINE ARTS is a newly established cultural organization in Porur, founded by Shri A.V. Manikantaan with the vision of promoting music and preserving traditional arts. The sabha was created to provide a platform for talented artists from around the world, offering them an opportunity to perform without any obligations. Shri A.V. Manikantaan, a renowned mridangam artist himself, aims to teach rhythm and music to young aspiring artists and share his deep knowledge of the art form with a broader audience.

In 2005, driven by this passion, Shri A.V. Manikantaan founded Jai Sathguru Sangeetha Vidyalaya in Adambakkam with just 10 students. Over the years, the school has grown exponentially and now boasts hundreds of students, with branches spread across South India.
Jai Sathguru Fine Arts in the Madanandapuram locality of Porur. The sabha began modestly, operating from a small space with a capacity of 30–40 people. Despite its size, the sabha has hosted a variety of cultural activities, including chamber concerts, lec-dems (lectures and demonstrations), competitions, and practice sessions.

In just three months, the response to the sabha has been overwhelmingly positive. Encouraged by this, the sabha is now looking to expand its reach to the general public.

To mark its new phase, Jai Sathguru Fine Arts hosted its first public concert at the Sri Durga Lakshmi Saraswati Temple in Santhosh Nagar, Madanandapuram, Chennai. The concert featured the esteemed Smt. Gayathri Venkatragavan, a star musician, whose performance was met with great appreciation. She was accompanied by the legendary violinist M.R. Gopinath, with Shri A.V. Manikantaan himself playing the mridangam, and Sri Sainath supporting on the ghatam.

The concert was attended by a large number of local music lovers who stayed throughout the performance, further solidifying the sabha's role in nurturing and promoting the musical arts in the community.

Jai Sathguru Fine Arts Sabha is steadily growing, and with its commitment to cultural enrichment and music education, it aims to become a prominent institution in the local and global arts scene.

A.V. Manikantaan , born in Chennai, comes from a distinguished musical lineage. His maternal grandfather, Sri S. Gopalakrishnan, was a renowned mridangam artist, and his mother, G. Subbulakshmi, is a respected music teacher and violinist. His brother, Chennai A. Srikanth Bhagavathar, is a prominent artist in the field of Namasankeerthanam.

Manikantaan began his musical journey in 1988 under the guidance of the late Shri Vaideeswaran Koil Vaidhyanatha Iyer, continuing until 1996. From 1997 onwards, he was fortunate to receive advanced training under the divine tutelage of Shri Guru Kaaraikkudi Mani.

In 2008, Manikantaan completed his Master’s degree in Rhythmology (Indian Music) from the University of Madras. He is an "A" Graded artist with All India Radio and a recipient of the Cultural Talent Search Scholarship under the scheme of the CCRT, New Delhi.
